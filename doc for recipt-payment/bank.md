1. Purpose

This protocol standardizes the Bank Reconciliation process to ensure accuracy in financial records, identify discrepancies between bank and system balances, and maintain complete integrity of cash accounts.

2. Scope

Applicable to all company bank accounts: checking, savings, long-term deposits, FX accounts, and POS settlement accounts.

3. Responsibilities

Treasury Officer: Perform reconciliation and record adjustments.

Financial Supervisor: Review and validate discrepancies.

Financial Manager: Approve major adjustments.

Internal Auditor: Periodic oversight.

4. Prerequisites

Access to bank statements (Excel/CSV/PDF).

Access to accounting/ERP system data.

Valid FX rate table (if applicable).

Prior-period adjustments must be finalized.

5. Process Steps
Step 1: Obtain Bank Statement

Retrieve full-period statement (start to end of month).

Verify completeness and accuracy.

Step 2: Standardize File Format

Convert bank file to a compatible format (Excel/CSV with Date, Description, Debit, Credit, Balance).

Ensure no duplicate or malformed entries exist.

Step 3: Import Statement into System

Upload file into Treasury/Bank module.

Initiate automatic reconciliation process.

Step 4: Auto-Matching

System matches transactions based on:

Equal amount

Close transaction dates

Similar descriptions

Matching reference/trace numbers (for POS)

Step 5: Identify Discrepancies

A) System-side discrepancies:

Incorrect amount registered

Missing entries

Duplicate entries

B) Bank-side discrepancies:

Incorrect bank charges

Unrecorded bank fees

Returned POS transactions

C) Timing differences:

Cheques cleared at bank but not recorded

POS settlements posted on the next day

Step 6: Adjustments

Record accounting entries for fees, interests, corrections, POS adjustments, etc.

Record FX revaluation entries (if applicable).

Update balances after supervisor approval.

Step 7: Final Review & Approval

Supervisor reviews discrepancy list.

Financial Manager approves adjustments.

Monthly reconciliation report is finalized and archived.

6. Outputs

Monthly Bank Reconciliation Report

Discrepancy list with resolution status

Adjustment journal entries

Signed reconciliation record

7. Frequency

Monthly (within 5 days after month-end)

For high-volume accounts: weekly or daily
